The Human Species

Posted on December 2nd, 2016, jsut after Summit@Sea 2016

People's lives, like rays of sunshine.
Streams of photons find their unique pathways.
Ancient DNA Origami conquistadors,
African tribes explorers,
Cryptolife sons of Orion, based in Monaco,
Transformational experiences in Oaxaca Mexico.
Private Space companies, Singularities,
Treehouse-builders, fancy oil vaporizers, cannabis gum!
Quantum computers, belief networks and AI workers.
Life is infinite in all it's forms, awe and wonder!
Feeling grateful to tangent such rich alien trajectories
of living, breathing photon streams of positive energies.
In a sip of coffee, I wonder what is it like to be the other.
Not that I fear I don't feel for my own path. What is the path?
When does the one get tired of looking for it?
When does the one decide to make their own?
Not that I fear I am not doing what I should be.
Epigenetic's, that's our curse and virtue!
Listening, that's my biggest hopeful wish for myself and the other!
Not that I fear the I which I write about is completely lost.
Entangled in its own net of words with uncountable meanings.
Just that this I got up on a Friday morning fascinated by the
infinitely complex and beautiful dance of trajectories.
Humans lives in this world and others.

Tuesdays

Posted on December 13, 2016, A Friday

My mind, a bird in a coffin with windows,
one for each mystic side, even some tunneling through time.
Feeling the betavoltaic clash of every single misty particle of life
as it collapses into the surface of my eyes, half open.
I see my neighbors, fellow alien forms of all energy forms.
Yes, Mr. Bones. Why, where, who... what happened?
My hearth, I wish I could hear it beating.
I hope it's in there, lost somewhere, a scintillating warrior.
Faced against its own battle in a battlefield, not well defined.
Against a distant tribe, In a world, not well described.
Armed with nothing but her beating hearth, the lioness hunts.
Transformed by the infinity in her, the omniscient hearth.
Life is much more than a line of code, a lion, locked in a coffin.

I've seen a house fly!

Posted on October 7th, 2016, Inspired by my boss

Windows dancing in the Sky.
The empty frames, like goasts swing between the clouds.
Am I the only one to see them? Am I mad to want to reach them,
Only to catch them and put them on the ground once more.

Who am I to judge the flying bricks, to say where’s up or down?
Am I not living in a house flying in the sky as well?
Looking down into the densely populated fractal awesomeness!!
Oh, how I love the view from here! …feeling dizzy in the swing.

Breath in once more, in the endless spin,
Trying to hold on to the dancing windows,
You see through the deadly water vortices at last.

No tear, no sound, no thought ...as you realize,
The only way away is to let the windows go.
Why struggle when you can enjoy their beauty in the sky.

SKY DANCE

by Bobi Rakova

Posted on January 11, 2016

Look up - blue skies, how many can you count?
Is it just one or is in an infinite number of horizons,
collapsing their power into your Soul, beginningless, endless.

A magnificent river of distant galaxies, looking at you now,
burning, setting everything on Fire. Ready, set, go -
You stair back, step into the battlefield! A cosmic fight of eternal energies.

You go on, only to feel how you change each other,
Energy is never lost, just redirected - invisible waterfalls between each and every.
You go on, playing in the vivid never-ending dance of energies.

Like a Samuel Becket play produced by ancient Japanese Noh monks,
the battle can never be described, never witnessed, never repeated.
Only felt from one's hearth as one smiles to the self within and without.

The colors of infinity, the fractal nature of our Souls.
Energy junkies, technomadic wonderers, we touch each others’ lives
like the raindrops touch the blossoms of a growing winter flower.

Maya

by Ralph Waldo Emerson

Posted on January 11, 2016

Illusion works impenetrable,
Weaving webs innumerable,
Her gay pictures never fail,
Crowds each on other, veil on veil,
Charmer who will be believed
By man who thirsts to be deceived.

Illusions like the tints of pearl,
Or changing colors of the sky,
Or ribbons of a dancing girl
That mend her beauty to the eye.

The cold gray down upon the quinces lieth
And the poor spinners weave their webs thereon
To share the sunshine that so spicy is.

Samson stark, at Dagon's knee,
Gropes for columns strong as he;
When his ringlets grew and curled,
Groped for axle of the world.

But Nature whistled with all her winds,
Did as she pleased and went her way.

If Life was a Fish Soup

by Bobi Rakova

Posted on January 11, 2016

Why would you need a wind tunnel when you have supercomputing?
That monster, which once was the top of all high tech inventions
a product of so much science, engineering, dreaming…
yet its magnificence in the distance strikes you as an Ocean of tensor dimensions.

Swim, swim little fish, swim in that Ocean of fictions.
As you alone and only you can find a way through.
Is it you, is it the flow… as if someone is giving directions?
But there’s the trap, there’s no way out - it’s a soup!
However hard you stir -
you might spill a bit, you might sip a bit only to end up back in the soup.

Why is it that it’s so hard to get out of the bowl of soup?
A leap of faith? There might be other bowls out there?
3 billion variances in the human genome
and yet just one bowl, my bowl…

If I was a fish I’d be a flying one. So that,
I can swim through the bowl of soup and then fly into the next one!
Millions, billions bowls of soup as if we’re not all made out of water,
as if we’re not all practically one(big bowl of fish soup)!
