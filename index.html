Bogdana Rakova

Social & environmenntal justice through response-able systems that empower participation and inclusion.


An engineer, ML researcher, community organizer, and founder, specifically focused on investigating and mitigating downstream risk of AI models, empowering marginalized populations, and encouraging social and environmental justice. She helped start the Responsible AI team at Accenture where she is a Data Scientist. She is a lead guest editor of the Springer Special Issue journal Intersections of Artificial Intelligence and Community Well-Being, as a research fellow at Partnership on AI she worked on disentangling the intersection of organizational structure and the work within the growing field of Responsible AI. She is passionate about the intersection of AI, community well-being, and environmental justice, also as part of the Happiness Alliance nonprofit.

Berkeley BIDS Computational Social Science Forum

April 26, 2021

A Relational View on Ethics and Technology: Bringing awareness to our inherent positionality, we gain new perspectives about the relational nature of (un)intended consequences of AI systems. Giving examples from a recent ethnographic study in the intersection of organizational structure and the work on ensuring the responsible development and use of AI, we investigate the so-called socio-technical context - the lived experience of some of the people actively involved in the AI ethics field. Learning from the field of Participatory Relational Design, we investigate what can we learn from the design of social movements (specifically in the Global South) about the way we work to ensure better alignment between AI systems and human and ecological well-being.
